SDU Market

The SDU market is popular among students for its variety of snacks and beverages, but its inefficiencies during peak hours—such as long queues, overcrowding, and slow checkouts—lead to customer frustration. This project aimed to redesign the shopping experience, focusing on minimizing wait times and improving satisfaction.

My role

For the project "Improving the Shopping Experience at SDU Market," I focused on creating sketches, prototypes, and wireframes. These served as the foundation for visualizing the redesigned shopping experience. My role also involved ensuring that the designs were user-friendly, visually appealing, and addressed the pain points identified during the research phase, such as overcrowding and long queues.

Specific Contributions

  • Sketches: I drafted initial ideas, visually representing potential solutions to improve the market's layout and processes.
  • Wireframes: I developed detailed wireframes to outline the structure of the redesign, ensuring clarity and functionality.
  • Prototypes: I created interactive prototypes, allowing team members and stakeholders to test and experience the proposed changes before implementation.

Problem Identification

Key challenges identified through interviews and observations:

  • Peak Hour Issues: Long queues (10-15 minutes) and congestion in narrow aisles.
  • Checkout Inefficiencies: Limited operational counters and frequent technical issues.
  • Abandoned Purchases: Customers often leave without completing purchases due to delays.

Proposed Solution

Postomat System: A system enabling students to pre-order items online, pay in advance, and retrieve purchases via QR codes at a designated locker.

  • Reduces queue times.
  • Simplifies checkout processes.
  • Enhances overall convenience.

Testing

Moderated RITE Testing Plan for Postomat System at SDU Market

The Postomat system at SDU Market aims to reduce queues by allowing students to order online, pay in advance, and pick up their purchases using a QR code. The testing will evaluate the usability of the online ordering system and the postomat pickup process, identifying any issues to improve user experience and minimize queues. The participants will include 5-7 members from the SDU community, both regular users and those unfamiliar with postomats. The prototypes include an online ordering platform (app/website) and the postomat interface for QR code scanning and order retrieval. Participants will complete the online ordering and pickup process, with metrics for task completion, time spent, errors, and feedback. The RITE methodology will be used, with initial tests identifying issues and successive rounds of testing to improve the prototypes. The expected results are reduced wait times, an intuitive system, and positive user feedback on convenience. Data will be collected through quantitative measures of time and errors, and qualitative feedback on the overall user experience.

Final Design

Updates and Adjustments After Testing:

  • Self-Service Ordering System Changes: Self-service checkout stations were removed to simplify the process. All customers will now use regular cash registers for a smoother checkout.
  • Manager Interface Changes: The QR code tab was removed, and the system now automatically opens the QR page after scanning, streamlining the admin workflow.

What I learn?

From this project, I learned the importance of user-centered design and incorporating feedback from real users into the design process. I also gained a deeper understanding of aligning design elements with project goals to solve specific problems effectively.

This experience taught me to approach future projects with an iterative mindset—starting with low-fidelity designs and refining them based on feedback. It also emphasized the importance of usability testing and collaboration to ensure a design not only looks good but also works well. These insights have influenced how I plan and execute tasks in subsequent projects, ensuring a more structured and user-focused workflow.
