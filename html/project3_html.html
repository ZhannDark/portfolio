Project Overview

The goal is to improve a sleep-tracking app tailored for students, integrating behavioral change techniques and data from wearable devices. The app aims to address issues like irregular sleep patterns, stress, and phone distractions, helping students build healthier sleep habits.

My Role

In the Sleep Tracking App project, my role was focused on background research and data collection. I analyzed the sleep habits of university students and conducted interviews to understand their challenges and preferences. I collected key insights, such as irregular sleep patterns, stress, and the impact of late-night phone usage, which were critical in shaping the direction of the project. These findings informed the improvements made to the existing Tide app, ensuring it addressed real user needs. Specifically, I designed and conducted interviews, compiled data on user behaviors, and identified essential features like AI-driven insights, reminders, and distraction-blocking tools. My work helped pinpoint the importance of integrating smartwatches and providing actionable feedback to users.

  • Research: Conducted interviews and observations to understand student sleep habits and challenges.
  • Design: Developed wireframes and interactive prototypes for user onboarding and sleep tracking features.
  • Testing: Participated in moderated usability testing to refine the app design and functionality.

Background

Many university students struggle with poor sleep habits due to stress, busy schedules, and phone distractions. The goal of the project is to create a smartwatch-compatible app that provides personalized tips, reminders, and insights to help students build better sleep routines.

  • Sleep Habits: Irregular schedules, stress, and late-night phone use are common issues.
  • Awareness: Few students use existing apps like Sleep Cycle,Meditopia due to lack of motivation.
  • User Needs: Students prefer features like reminders, insights, and rewards for healthy sleep.
  • Concerns: Privacy and data accuracy are critical.

Mental Model Diagram

  • Sleep Irregularity: Students struggle with inconsistent bedtimes due to homework and exams, causing fatigue and poor concentration.
  • Stress and Mental Health: Stress and anxiety about studies and personal life disrupt sleep, making it hard to relax and fall asleep.
  • Technology Distractions: Using phones before bed leads to staying awake longer or feeling anxious, affecting sleep quality.

Testing and Insights

Competitive Testing

We chose competitive testing for our app to evaluate its features against similar sleep tracking apps like Sleep Cycle and Meditopia. This approach allowed us to understand the strengths and weaknesses of competing applications and identify areas where our app could offer a better experience, particularly for students. By analyzing their functionality, we were able to pinpoint missing features that were important to our target audience, such as social media blocking, AI-driven sleep analytics, and wearable integration. Additionally, we gathered feedback from student users to further refine our design and features. Their insights helped us prioritize the inclusion of real-time engagement elements, such as push notifications for sleep reminders, and streamlined the user interface for a more intuitive experience. This testing process was key to ensuring our app addressed student-specific needs and provided meaningful improvements to their sleep habits.
