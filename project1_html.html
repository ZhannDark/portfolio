Problems Students Face

To understand the real challenges students face on campus, we conducted interviews with students from various backgrounds. These conversations helped us uncover their main pain point: the difficulty in finding free study rooms. By engaging directly with potential users, we gained valuable insights into their daily experiences and needs.

My role

For the SDU Scout Robot project, I was involved in creating sketches and a storyboard to visualize how the robot would interact with students. These visuals helped us communicate our design ideas clearly to the team and make decisions about the user experience. Each team member also contributed by conducting interviews with students and analyzing the collected data to understand their needs better.

Data Analysis

Using the information collected from the interviews, we organized the data into an affinity diagram to identify recurring themes and group similar issues. This helped us create scenarios representing typical user experiences, like students struggling to find study rooms during exams.

Sketch

We also created a sketch of the robot to visualize its design and appearance. The sketch focuses on how the robot will look, giving a clear representation of its physical form and key features.

Storyboards

A group of students is looking for a study room during the afternoon, but they aren't sure which rooms are free.

Evaluation Testing

We tested the prototype with three students, focusing on finding available study rooms. Their feedback helped us improve the design, ensuring it was simple and effective. This experience influenced my approach to the next project by helping me focus more on user experience from the start. I understood that user feedback and testing are essential for developing a system that meets real needs. In our future work, I plan to incorporate these lessons by focusing more on creating clear visual aids and ensuring that every feature addresses actual user problems, based on thorough research and testing.

What I learn?

Through this project, I learned how to combine design with functionality to create a user-friendly solution. I also realized how important it is to ensure that the design not only looks good but is practical for real-life use. Working on the storyboard taught me how to visualize a user’s journey, which helped us refine the robot's interactions and responses.
